# Information Security Governance & Regulation Notes

## Important Terminologies
- **Governance**: Managing and directing an organisation or system to achieve objectives and ensure compliance with laws, regulations, and standards.  
- **Regulation**: A rule or law enforced by a governing body to ensure compliance and protect against harm.  
- **Compliance**: Adhering to laws, regulations, and standards applicable to an organisation or system.  

## Information Security Governance
- Represents an organisation's structure, policies, methods, and guidelines to ensure privacy, reliability, and accessibility of information assets.  
- Essential due to increasing cyber threats for:
  - Risk management  
  - Protecting confidential data from unauthorised access  
  - Regulatory adherence  
- Managed by top-tier management.

### Processes of Information Security Governance
1. **Strategy**: Align information security strategy with business objectives.  
2. **Policies & Procedures**: Define rules for use and protection of information assets.  
3. **Risk Management**: Conduct risk assessments and implement mitigation measures.  
4. **Performance Measurement**: Use metrics/KPIs to measure governance effectiveness.  
5. **Compliance**: Ensure adherence to relevant regulations and best practices.  

## Information Security Regulation
- Legal and regulatory frameworks governing information asset protection.  
- Focused on preventing unauthorized access, theft, and misuse.  
- Compliance is mandatory and enforced by government/regulatory bodies.  

### Examples of Regulations/Standards
- **GDPR**: General Data Protection Regulation  
- **PCI DSS**: Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard  
- **PIPEDA**: Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act  

## Key Benefits of Governance & Regulation
- **Robust Security Posture**: Reduces risk of breaches and data theft.  
- **Increased Stakeholder Confidence**: Builds trust by demonstrating serious cybersecurity measures.  
- **Regulatory Compliance**: Avoids legal/financial penalties and reputational damage.  
- **Better Business Alignment**: Ensures security strategies are cost-effective and support business goals.  
- **Informed Decision-Making**: Provides knowledge for risk-aware security decisions.  
- **Competitive Advantage**: Demonstrates commitment to data protection and trustworthiness.  

## Relevant Laws & Regulations

| Law/Regulation | Domain | Description |
|----------------|--------|------------|
| **GDPR** | Data Privacy & Protection | EU regulation for handling and protecting personal data of EU citizens/residents. |
| **HIPAA** | Healthcare | US law ensuring sensitivity and protection of health-related information. |
| **PCI DSS** | Financial | Sets technical/operational standards for secure handling of cardholder data. |
| **GLBA** | Financial | Requires financial companies to protect nonpublic personal information (NPI) and implement security programs. |
